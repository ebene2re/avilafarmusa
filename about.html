About Avila Farm USA

Avila Farm USA is a generational American gamefowl heritage farm based in Texas, built on values passed down long before the digital age.

Our story begins in the early 19th century, when a Spanish-speaking family from Mexico migrated to the United States in search of opportunity and stability. Upon settling in Texas, they found more than land ‚Äî they found a lifelong connection to the American gamefowl culture. What began as admiration soon became dedication, discipline, and mastery.

Over generations, that passion evolved into responsible breeding, structured care, and a deep respect for the birds and the culture surrounding them. Each generation carried forward not just bloodlines, but principles: patience, honesty, and accountability.

Today, Avila Farm USA continues that legacy with modern standards and full compliance with U.S. federal regulations governing poultry and gamefowl. We operate transparently, prioritize ethical breeding practices, and maintain structured housing and daily care routines designed to support health and proper development.

We do not believe heritage is something to be claimed loudly ‚Äî it is something to be proven quietly, over time. Our farm exists to preserve tradition, protect integrity within the gamefowl community, and serve breeders who value authenticity over shortcuts.

Avila Farm USA stands as a living bridge between generations ‚Äî honoring the past, operating responsibly in the present, and protecting the future of American gamefowl heritage.

Our Journey

Early 1900s
Family migration from Mexico to Texas. First connection to American gamefowl culture established.
1987
Avila Farm USA officially founded. Formalized breeding program begins with structured documentation and bloodline preservation.
1995-2010
Expansion of breeding operations. Introduction of advanced health protocols and facility modernization.
2010-Present
Third generation takes leadership. Full NPIP certification achieved. Digital presence established while maintaining traditional values.
